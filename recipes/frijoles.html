Peruvian Beans

Creamy Peruvian Beans

These beans are cooked from dry peruvian (or mayacoba) beans. No need to soak overnight. Simply place them in water and season and cook for 90mins to 2 hours. They make such creamy beans you may never want to eat Pinto Beans again.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry peruvian beans.
  • Salt to taste (~1tbsp).
  • 1 tsp cominos (cumin seeds)
  • 2 or 3 garlic cloves (optional)
  1. Rinse the beans twice, and remove any stones or nasty beans.
  2. In a pot, cover beans with 10 cups of water.
  3. Add cominos and garlic.
  4. Bring to a boil, and reduce to a strong simmer. After around 45 minutes, add the salt and taste the water until it is your desired saltiness level.
  5. Continue cooking until beans are soft and tender, usually another hour, fir a total cook time of 90 minutes to 2 hours.
