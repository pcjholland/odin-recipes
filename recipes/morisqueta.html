Morisqueta

Rice topped with pork simmered in a spicy guajillo chile and tomato sauce

Pork shoulder braised in a vibrant tomato-gaujillo chile sauce. But, where is the Morisqueta?

When I first encountered this dish, the above image was used to display the finished product. The primordial red hue of the sauce was startling and appealing. I was excited to make it.

This dish is a regional Mexican dish and - judging by the picture - you might think Morisqueta is the deep red sauce and pork. However, Morisqueta is actually slang for rice. This sauce is just a Michoachan riff on the rice dish.

Morisqueta Michocana, when done right is a cheap and decadent rice dish, topped with braised pork in a spicy tomato chile sauce. The sauce has incredible depth of flavor. If you are new to mexican cooking you will really enjoy experiencing the creation and consumption of this dish!

Ingredients

  • 5-6 Guajillo Chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2-3 Chile De Arbol, stemmed
  • 7 Large Roma tomatoes
  • Half a white onion
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 Bay leaf
  • 1 cup homemade chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon morton kosher salt
  • 1 tsp Oregano
  • 1 tsp ground pepper
  • 3 lbs Pork shoulder, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • Rendered lard or olive oil
  • Rice and beans, leftvoers okay

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees
  2. Using gloves, split the guajillo peppers open and scrap out the seeds, removing the stem. Set aside with the stemless chile de arbol.
  3. Core the roma tomatoes and set on a tray with the onion. PLace in oven and cook until the tomatoes are beginning to char
  4. Place the chiles, garlic, salt, pepper, oregano, bay leaf and the stock into a small pot. Bring to a boil and immediately remove from heat, letting the ingredients steep in the stock for 30 minutes, covered.
  5. Blend the tomaotes, onion and contents for the stock pot into a smooth red sauce.
  6. Heat 2-4 tablespoons of lard in a large heavy pot. Sear the pork, in batches, browning all sides. Remove pork and set aside. Do not hesitate to add more lard for the final few batches.
  7. In the same pot, without cleaning the pork fat and crispy bits, add the tomato chile sauce. It should be bubbling. Use a wooden spoon to deglaze the pot.
  8. After ~3-5 minutes, add the pork back in, bring the pot to a simmer, and cover for 60-90 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Serve over rice and beans. Include fresh corn tortillas if able.
