Buttery White Rice

White rice with butter and chopped corn, carrots and peas

This rice is not the usual red spanish rice found in Mexican cooking. Instead, we get a savory buttery rice that highlights chile sauces it is served with.

This rice is best served with Carrots, Corn and Peppers (such as poblano) all diced into half centimeter chunks. However, chopping the carrots nd poblano yourself can be labor intensive. So, instead, this can be made with a bag of frozen diced carrots and peas, with another bag of frozen corn. The recipe assumes you have a bag of frozen peas and carrots and some frozen corn (not canned).

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long grain white rice, such as basmati or jasmine
  • 3 cups of chicken stock, or 3 cups of prepared Knorr Suiza or other chicken bouillon
  • 1.5 cups frozen pea and carrots medley
  • 1.5 cups frozen corn, preferably white corn
  • 1 clove of garlic, pressed, grated, or minced
  • 4tbps butter (half a stick)
  • 2 tsps kosher salt

Steps

  1. Add Butter to pan. Get hot, but not browning.
  2. Add veg, garlic and salt
  3. Saute for 6-8 minutes, until frozen veg is hit, and beginning to brown. Look for the water that the veg evaoprates out to begin to cook off, leaving only butter in the bottom.
  4. Add the rice and cook, until beginnning to become golden.
  5. Add the chick stock. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer and cover with lid.
  6. Cook 20 minutes, then chck to make sure the liquid is absorbed. Cook for another 5-10 if still liquidy, otherwise, take off heat and let sit for 10 minutes
